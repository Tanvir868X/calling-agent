Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) – Medical Customer Service
Appointments & Scheduling
Q: How do I schedule an appointment?
A: You can schedule an appointment by calling our office during business hours, booking online through our website, or using our patient portal.

Q: Do I need a referral to make an appointment?
A: Some specialists require a referral from a primary care provider. Please check with your insurance provider or call us directly for guidance.

Q: What should I do if I need to cancel or reschedule my appointment?
A: Please notify us at least 24 hours in advance to cancel or reschedule. Late cancellations or no-shows may result in a fee.

Insurance & Billing
Q: Do you accept my insurance?
A: We accept most major insurance plans. Contact us or check our website for a full list of accepted providers.

Q: What if I don’t have insurance?
A: We offer self-pay options and payment plans for uninsured patients. Please contact our billing department for details.

Q: How can I get a copy of my bill or ask about a charge?
A: You can access billing information through the patient portal or contact our billing team directly.

Medical Records & Privacy
Q: How can I request my medical records?
A: Submit a signed records release form via our portal, email, or in person. Please allow up to 7 business days for processing.

Q: Is my personal health information kept confidential?
A: Yes. We comply with HIPAA regulations to ensure your health information is secure and confidential.

Prescriptions & Refills
Q: How do I request a prescription refill?
A: Refill requests can be made through your pharmacy, our patient portal, or by calling our office during business hours.

Q: How long does it take to process a refill?
A: Refill requests are typically processed within 24–48 hours, excluding weekends and holidays.

Office Policies & Services
Q: What should I bring to my appointment?
A: Please bring a valid ID, your insurance card, a list of medications, and any relevant medical records or referrals.

Q: Do you offer telemedicine appointments?
A: Yes. Virtual visits are available for many services. Call us or book online to schedule a telehealth appointment.

Q: What if I have a medical emergency?
A: If you are experiencing a life-threatening emergency, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room. For urgent but non-emergency concerns, contact our office during business hours.
